About

On April 29th, Princeton TruckFest will bring together Princeton University students and the local Princeton community to help support some great causes (stay tuned for our press release of these causes shortly!). This charity event is being hosted by the Community Service Inter-Club Council, in conjunction with the Pace Council for Civic Values.

Prospect Avenue will be closed from Washington Road to Olden Street from 1-5 PM while over 15 food trucks parked in front of the eating clubs serve their signature items.

Mercer Street Friends

Proceeds from TruckFest 2016 will support the Send Hunger Packing Initiative , a collaboration between the Mercer Street Friends Food Bank and the Trenton Area Soup Kitchen (TASK) , which provides low-income schoolchildren with nutritious meal packs for the weekends. Though Princeton is considered a prosperous community, one in eight students in the Princeton public schools are eligible for free or reduced-tickets school lunches, and can thus participate in this program.

Meals on Wheels

Proceeds from TruckFest 2016 will support Meals on Wheels of Trenton/Ewing , a non-profit organization that provides nutritious meals and other services to homebound locals in efforts to promote their independence, dignity, health and wellbeing. The consequences of child and adult hunger are very serious. Children from families that struggle with food insecurity are more likely to do poorly on tests, not attend class, and have difficulty getting along with their peers. Princeton TruckFest recognizes the severity of this social issue and wants to contribute to the fight against it.
